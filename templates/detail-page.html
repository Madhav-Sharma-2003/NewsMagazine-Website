The ICC Champions Trophy 2025 concluded with India emerging victorious against New Zealand in a thrilling final at Dubai International Stadium. Chasing a target of 252, India held their nerve to secure a four-wicket win, powered by Rohit Sharma’s composed 76 runs. The triumph marked India’s third Champions Trophy title, reinforcing their dominance in world cricket. The match showcased resilience, strategy, and skill, leaving fans with unforgettable memories of a hard-fought contest between two top teams.

The ICC Champions Trophy 2025 final between India and New Zealand was billed as a clash of titans. Both teams had shown remarkable consistency throughout the tournament, with India’s batting depth and New Zealand’s disciplined bowling setting the stage for a thrilling finale. Fans across the globe tuned in, anticipating a contest that would define the future of ODI cricket. Batting first, New Zealand posted a competitive total of 251/7 in their 50 overs. Daryl Mitchell anchored the innings with a patient 63, while Michael Bracewell’s late fireworks added crucial runs. Despite their efforts, India’s spinners managed to keep the scoring rate in check, ensuring the target remained within reach.
